# Tickertape Screener Parameters - Comprehensive Analysis Guide

## Overview

This document organizes the 200+ stock analysis parameters available in Tickertape's screener into logical clusters. The first four fundamental segments are covered in detail:

1. **Profitability** - How efficiently a company generates profits
2. **Financial Ratios** - Operational efficiency and financial health
3. **Growth** - Revenue, earnings, and cash flow trajectory
4. **Valuation** - Is the stock fairly priced?

---

## Segment 1: PROFITABILITY

**Purpose:** Measures how efficiently a company converts resources (capital, assets, revenue) into profits.

### Parameter Summary Table

| Parameter | Formula/Definition | What It Tells You | Good Sign |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **ROCE** | EBIT ÷ Capital Employed | Profitability per unit of capital invested | Higher is better; shows resource efficiency |
| **Cost of Goods Sold** | Direct costs of production | Manufacturing/production efficiency | Lower % of revenue = better margins |
| **Cash Flow Margin** | Cash from Operations ÷ Revenue | Cash generation efficiency | Higher = quality earnings |
| **5Y Avg Cash Flow Margin** | 5-year average of above | Consistent cash generation ability | Stable or improving trend |
| **Return on Equity (ROE)** | Net Profit ÷ Avg Shareholders' Equity | Profit per rupee of shareholder investment | >15% generally good; trending up |
| **5Y Avg ROE** | 5-year average ROE | Consistency of shareholder returns | Stable high ROE = quality business |
| **Return on Assets (ROA)** | Net Profit ÷ Avg Total Assets | How well assets are used to generate profit | Higher = efficient asset utilization |
| **5Y Avg ROA** | 5-year average ROA | Long-term asset efficiency | Smooth, improving trend preferred |
| **Net Profit Margin** | Net Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 | Rupees of profit per ₹100 revenue | Higher margin = pricing power |
| **5Y Avg Net Profit Margin** | 5-year average of above | Sustainable profitability | Stable/improving = operational excellence |
| **Return on Investment (ROI)** | Net Income ÷ (Equity + LT Debt + LT Liabilities) | Efficiency of total capital deployment | Higher = better capital allocation |
| **5Y Avg ROI** | 5-year average ROI | Long-term investment efficiency | Consistent high ROI |
| **Operating Profit Margin** | Operating Profit ÷ Revenue × 100 | Core business profitability | Higher = strong operations |
| **5Y Avg Operating Profit Margin** | 5-year average of above | Operational consistency | Stable/improving |

### Profitability Clusters

#### Cluster A: Return Metrics (Capital Efficiency)
These measure how efficiently the company uses different types of capital:

| Metric | Capital Base | Best For |
|--------|--------------|----------|
| ROE | Shareholder equity only | Equity investors |
| ROA | All assets | Asset-heavy businesses |
| ROCE | Debt + Equity capital | Overall efficiency |
| ROI | Total invested capital | Comparing capital allocation |

**Use Case:** Compare companies within the same sector. Higher returns with consistent trend = competitive advantage.

#### Cluster B: Margin Metrics (Profitability at Different Levels)
These show profit retention at each stage of the income statement:

| Metric | What It Captures | Key Insight |
|--------|-----------------|-------------|
| Operating Profit Margin | Core business efficiency | Before interest & taxes |
| Net Profit Margin | Final profitability | After all costs |
| Cash Flow Margin | Actual cash generated | Quality of earnings |

**Use Case:** Declining margins may signal pricing pressure or rising costs.

#### Cluster C: 5-Year Averages (Trend & Consistency)
All 5Y Avg metrics smooth out one-time items and show true underlying performance:
- Eliminates distortion from extraordinary gains/losses
- Easier comparison across multiple companies
- Shows if current performance is sustainable or an outlier

## Segment 2: FINANCIAL RATIOS

**Purpose:** Measures operational efficiency, liquidity, and financial health.

### Parameter Summary Table

| Parameter | Formula/Definition | What It Tells You | Good Sign |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **Working Capital Turnover** | Revenue ÷ Avg Working Capital | Sales generated per unit of working capital | Higher = efficient use of working capital |
| **Net Income / Liabilities** | Net Income ÷ Total Liabilities | Ability to service all debts | Higher = financially healthier |
| **Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)** | Avg days to pay suppliers | Cash management efficiency | Higher = retains cash longer (but not too high) |
| **Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)** | Avg days to collect from customers | Collection efficiency | Lower = faster cash conversion |
| **Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)** | Avg days to sell inventory | Inventory management | Lower = faster turnover (sector dependent) |
| **Asset Turnover Ratio** | Revenue ÷ Avg Total Assets | Revenue per unit of assets | Higher = efficient asset use |
| **Inventory Turnover Ratio** | COGS ÷ Avg Inventory | Times inventory is sold & replaced | Higher = strong sales/efficient inventory |
| **Earning Power** | EBIT ÷ Total Assets | Profit generation ability | Higher = stronger earning capacity |
| **Long Term Debt to Equity** | LT Debt ÷ Shareholders' Equity | Long-term leverage | Lower = less financial risk |
| **Debt to Equity** | Total Debt ÷ Shareholders' Equity | Overall leverage | Lower generally better; sector dependent |
| **Quick Ratio** | (Current Assets - Inventory) ÷ Current Liabilities | Immediate liquidity | >1 = can meet short-term obligations |
| **Current Ratio** | Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities | Short-term solvency | 1-2 is optimal; extremes are concerning |
| **Cash Conversion Cycle** | DIO + DSO - DPO | Days to convert inventory to cash | Lower = faster cash generation |
| **Interest Coverage Ratio** | EBIT ÷ Interest Expense | Ability to pay interest | >3 = comfortable; <1 = danger |

### Financial Ratios Clusters

#### Cluster A: Liquidity Ratios (Can the company pay its bills?)

| Ratio | Strictness | Interpretation |
|-------|-----------|----------------|
| Current Ratio | Moderate | Includes inventory |
| Quick Ratio | Strict | Excludes inventory (more conservative) |

**Use Case:** Banks look at these ratios. Quick Ratio <1 may signal cash crunch.

#### Cluster B: Efficiency Ratios (How well is the business running?)

| Ratio | Measures | Industry Context |
|-------|----------|------------------|
| Asset Turnover | Revenue vs assets | Retail high, infra low |
| Inventory Turnover | Sales vs inventory | FMCG high, auto low |
| Working Capital Turnover | Sales vs working capital | Higher = lean operations |

**Use Case:** Compare against sector peers; significant deviation needs investigation.

#### Cluster C: Cash Cycle Metrics (Operating Efficiency)

| Component | Impact on Cash | Goal |
|-----------|---------------|------|
| DIO (Inventory) | Longer = more cash tied up | Minimize |
| DSO (Receivables) | Longer = slower collection | Minimize |
| DPO (Payables) | Longer = retain cash longer | Optimize (not too long) |
| **Cash Conversion Cycle** | DIO + DSO - DPO | Lower is better |

**Use Case:** Negative CCC (like Amazon) = business generates cash before paying suppliers.

#### Cluster D: Leverage & Solvency Ratios (Financial Risk)

| Ratio | Risk Level Indicator |
|-------|---------------------|
| Debt to Equity | Overall debt burden |
| LT Debt to Equity | Long-term obligations |
| Interest Coverage | Ability to service debt |
| Net Income / Liabilities | Overall solvency |

**Use Case:** High leverage + low interest coverage = risky. Banks & NBFCs naturally have higher D/E.

## Segment 3: GROWTH

**Purpose:** Measures the trajectory of key business metrics over time.

### Parameter Summary Table

| Parameter | Formula/Definition | What It Tells You | Good Sign |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **1Y Forward Revenue Growth** | (Est. Revenue - Actual Revenue) ÷ Actual × 100 | Expected revenue increase | Positive = business expanding |
| **1Y Historical Revenue Growth** | YoY revenue change | Actual revenue trajectory | Consistent double-digit growth |
| **5Y Historical Revenue Growth** | Revenue CAGR over 5 years | Long-term growth trend | Sustained high CAGR |
| **1Y Forward EBITDA Growth** | Expected EBITDA change | Operating profit trajectory | Should match/exceed revenue growth |
| **1Y Historical EBITDA Growth** | YoY EBITDA change | Operating efficiency trend | Higher than revenue growth = expanding margins |
| **5Y Historical EBITDA Growth** | EBITDA CAGR over 5 years | Long-term operational growth | Stable or accelerating |
| **1Y Forward EPS Growth** | Expected EPS change | Profit per share trajectory | Higher = value creation |
| **1Y Historical EPS Growth** | YoY EPS change | Earnings efficiency | Should outpace revenue growth |
| **5Y Historical EPS Growth** | EPS CAGR over 5 years | Long-term shareholder returns | Consistent >15% is excellent |
| **1Y Forward Operating Cash Flow Growth** | Expected CFO change | Future cash generation | Positive = sustainable growth |
| **1Y Historical Operating Cash Flow Growth** | YoY CFO change | Actual cash generation ability | Growing faster than earnings |
| **5Y Historical Operating Cash Flow Growth** | CFO CAGR over 5 years | Long-term cash creation | Should track or exceed profit growth |
| **3Y Historical Dividend Growth** | DPS CAGR over 3 years | Dividend trajectory | Growing dividends = confident management |

### Growth Clusters

#### Cluster A: Revenue Growth (Top-line Expansion)

| Timeframe | Metric | Purpose |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Forward (1Y) | Expected | Market expectations |
| Historical (1Y) | Actual YoY | Recent momentum |
| Historical (5Y) | CAGR | Sustainable growth rate |

**Use Case:** Revenue growth without profit growth = value destruction. Watch margin trends.

#### Cluster B: Profitability Growth (Bottom-line Quality)

| Metric | What It Shows |
|--------|---------------|
| EBITDA Growth | Operating leverage |
| EPS Growth | Per-share value creation |
| Operating Cash Flow Growth | Cash quality of growth |

**Use Case:** If EPS growth > Revenue growth = improving margins (positive). If EPS growth < Revenue growth = margin compression (investigate).

#### Cluster C: Forward vs Historical (Expectations vs Reality)

| Comparison | Interpretation |
|------------|---------------|
| Forward > Historical | Market expects acceleration |
| Forward < Historical | Deceleration expected |
| Forward negative | Growth concerns |

**Use Case:** If forward metrics are consistently wrong vs historical, market may be mispricing the stock.

#### Cluster D: Dividend Growth (Shareholder Returns)

| Metric | Signal |
|--------|--------|
| 3Y Dividend Growth | Management's confidence in cash flows |
| Growing dividends | Stable, mature business |
| No dividends | Either reinvesting for growth or financially weak |

**Use Case:** Dividend growth + low payout ratio = room for future increases.

## Segment 4: VALUATION

**Purpose:** Determines if the stock price is justified by fundamentals.

### Parameter Summary Table

| Parameter | Formula/Definition | What It Tells You | Good Sign |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|-----------|
| **PE Ratio** | Price ÷ EPS | Price per unit of earnings | Lower vs peers may indicate undervaluation |
| **Forward PE Ratio** | Price ÷ Estimated EPS | Expected earnings multiple | Forward PE < Current PE = growth expected |
| **PE Premium vs Sector** | (Stock PE ÷ Sector PE) - 1 | Relative valuation | Negative = potential opportunity |
| **PE Premium vs Sub-sector** | (Stock PE ÷ Sub-sector PE) - 1 | Niche comparison | More precise than sector |
| **PB Ratio** | Price ÷ Book Value per Share | Price vs net assets | Lower = potentially undervalued (esp. for banks) |
| **PB Premium vs Sector** | (Stock PB ÷ Sector PB) - 1 | Relative asset valuation | Negative = discount |
| **PB Premium vs Sub-sector** | (Stock PB ÷ Sub-sector PB) - 1 | Niche asset comparison | Better for similar businesses |
| **PS Ratio** | Price ÷ Revenue per Share | Price vs sales | Useful for loss-making companies |
| **Forward PS Ratio** | Price ÷ Estimated Revenue per Share | Expected revenue multiple | Lower = potential value |
| **PS Premium vs Sector** | (Stock PS ÷ Sector PS) - 1 | Revenue multiple comparison | Negative = underpriced |
| **PS Premium vs Sub-sector** | (Stock PS ÷ Sub-sector PS) - 1 | Niche revenue comparison | More relevant |
| **Dividend Yield** | DPS ÷ Price × 100 | Cash return from dividends | Higher = income; but too high = risk |
| **Dividend Yield vs Sector** | Stock Yield - Sector Yield | Relative income potential | Positive = better income |
| **Dividend Yield vs Sub-sector** | Stock Yield - Sub-sector Yield | Niche income comparison | Context matters |
| **Price / CFO** | Market Cap ÷ Cash from Operations | Price vs operating cash | Lower = cash-rich at good price |
| **Price / Free Cash Flow** | Market Cap ÷ FCF | Price vs distributable cash | Lower = better value |
| **EV / EBITDA** | Enterprise Value ÷ EBITDA | Total value vs operating profit | Lower = potentially undervalued |
| **EV / Revenue** | Enterprise Value ÷ Revenue | Total value vs sales | Useful for high-growth, unprofitable companies |
| **EV / EBIT** | Enterprise Value ÷ EBIT | Total value vs operating income | More conservative than EV/EBITDA |
| **EV / Free Cash Flow** | Enterprise Value ÷ FCF | Total value vs cash generation | Lower = better deal for acquirer |
| **EV / Invested Capital** | Enterprise Value ÷ Invested Capital | Valuation vs capital base | High = susceptible to competition |
| **Enterprise Value** | Market Cap + Debt - Cash | Total acquisition cost | Context for EV-based ratios |

### Valuation Clusters

#### Cluster A: Earnings-Based Multiples (Most Common)

| Ratio | Best For | Limitation |
|-------|----------|------------|
| PE Ratio | Profitable companies | Doesn't work for loss-makers |
| Forward PE | Growth companies | Depends on estimate accuracy |
| PB Ratio | Banks, NBFCs, asset-heavy | Doesn't capture intangibles |

**Use Case:** Compare PE within same sector. Lower PE + high growth = opportunity. Lower PE + declining fundamentals = value trap.

#### Cluster B: Enterprise Value Multiples (Debt-Adjusted)

| Ratio | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|
| EV/EBITDA | Most versatile; includes debt |
| EV/EBIT | More conservative (includes depreciation) |
| EV/Revenue | Loss-making growth companies |
| EV/FCF | Cash-focused valuation |

**Use Case:** EV multiples account for debt, making them better for comparing companies with different capital structures.

#### Cluster C: Cash Flow Multiples (Quality Check)

| Ratio | Focus |
|-------|-------|
| Price/CFO | Operating cash generation |
| Price/FCF | Distributable cash |
| EV/FCF | Enterprise-level cash generation |

**Use Case:** If P/E is low but P/FCF is high, earnings may not be converting to cash (red flag).

#### Cluster D: Premium/Discount Analysis (Relative Valuation)

| Comparison Level | Use When |
|------------------|----------|
| vs Sector | Broad comparison |
| vs Sub-sector | Similar business models |

**Interpretation:**
- Negative premium = Stock trading at discount
- Positive premium = Stock trading at premium
- Premium should be justified by superior fundamentals

**Use Case:** Stock at 30% PE discount to sector with same growth = potential opportunity. Stock at 50% premium with similar metrics = potentially overvalued.

#### Cluster E: Income/Yield Metrics (For Income Investors)

| Metric | Investor Type |
|--------|--------------|
| Dividend Yield | Income-focused |
| Yield vs Sector | Relative income |

**Use Case:** Very high yield may signal: (a) stock price collapsed due to problems, or (b) genuine income opportunity. Investigate the cause.

## Cross-Segment Analysis Framework

### The Complete Health Check

| Dimension | Key Metrics | Question Answered |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Profitability** | ROE, ROA, ROCE, Margins | Is the business profitable? |
| **Financial Health** | D/E, Interest Coverage, Current Ratio | Is it financially stable? |
| **Growth** | Revenue CAGR, EPS CAGR, CFO Growth | Is it growing? |
| **Valuation** | PE, EV/EBITDA, PB | Is the price right? |

### Red Flag Checklist

| Segment | Red Flag | Signal |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Profitability | Declining ROE + rising debt | Inefficient capital allocation |
| Financial Ratios | Interest Coverage <1 | Cannot pay interest from operations |
| Growth | Negative EPS growth with positive revenue growth | Margin collapse |
| Valuation | Very low PE with declining fundamentals | Value trap |

### Green Flag Checklist

| Segment | Green Flag | Signal |
|---------|-----------|--------|
| Profitability | ROE >15% consistently | Competitive moat |
| Financial Ratios | Improving Cash Conversion Cycle | Operational excellence |
| Growth | EPS CAGR > Revenue CAGR | Expanding margins |
| Valuation | Low PE + high growth + strong ROE | Potential multibagger |

## Quick Reference: When to Use Which Metric

| If You Want To... | Use These Metrics |
|-------------------|-------------------|
| Find quality businesses | ROE, ROCE, Operating Margins |
| Check financial safety | D/E, Interest Coverage, Current Ratio |
| Identify growth stocks | EPS CAGR, Revenue CAGR, Forward PE |
| Find undervalued stocks | PE vs Sector, EV/EBITDA, PB Ratio |
| Find income stocks | Dividend Yield, Payout Ratio |
| Assess turnaround potential | Cash Flow Margin, Working Capital Turnover |
| Compare across sectors | EV/EBITDA, ROE, ROCE |

## Appendix: Sector-Specific Considerations

### Banking & NBFC
- **Primary:** PB Ratio, ROE, NPA ratios (not in screener)
- **Avoid:** PE (less relevant), Asset Turnover (different meaning)

### IT Services
- **Primary:** PE, ROE, EPS Growth, Cash Flow Margins
- **Consider:** Employee cost metrics, revenue per employee

### Manufacturing
- **Primary:** Asset Turnover, Inventory Days, Operating Margins
- **Consider:** Capacity utilization proxies, ROCE

### Consumer Goods
- **Primary:** Gross Margins, Inventory Turnover, Working Capital
- **Consider:** Brand-driven premium vs commodity players

### Real Estate
- **Primary:** PB Ratio, Debt/Equity, Cash Flows
- **Avoid:** Traditional PE (project-based revenue recognition)
