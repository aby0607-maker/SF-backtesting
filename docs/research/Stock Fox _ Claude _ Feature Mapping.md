## **A. STOCK ANALYSIS & RESEARCH ENGINE**

### **A8: Historical Trend Analysis (5-10 year analysis for context on metrics)**

**My Understanding:** This is about showing how a metric has evolved over time, not just the current snapshot.

**Example:**

* Current ROE: 18.3%  
* But Historical Context shows: ROE trend over 5 years: 12% → 14% → 16% → 17% → 18.3% (improving trajectory)  
* Or conversely: ROE was 25% five years ago, now 18.3% (declining trajectory)

**Why it matters:** A metric in isolation can be misleading. 18% ROE looks good, but if it was 25% and declining, that's a red flag. If it was 12% and improving, that's a positive signal.

**How it manifests in product:** Within each metric display, there's a mini-trend visualization or "vs 5Y avg" context line.

---

### **A13 & A14: Quick Analysis Mode vs Full Analysis Mode**

**My Understanding:**

| Aspect | Quick Analysis | Full Analysis |
| ----- | ----- | ----- |
| **Use Case** | Validation \- "I heard about this stock, is it worth digging deeper?" | Deep Dive \- "I want to understand everything before investing" |
| **Time** | 2-3 minutes | 10-15 minutes |
| **Output** | Overall score \+ top 3 signals (positive/negative) \+ verdict | All 11 segments with drill-down \+ full evidence \+ personalized verdict |
| **User State** | Exploring, filtering, deciding what to research | Committed to researching this specific stock |

**Your Question: Shouldn't the Scorecard allow for both?**

Yes \- this could be a **single Scorecard artifact with expandable depth** rather than two separate modes:

* Default view \= Quick (score \+ key signals)  
* Expand \= Full (all segments, all metrics)

**Decision Point:** Do we want:

* **Option A:** One Scorecard with progressive disclosure (expand/collapse)  
* **Option B:** Two distinct entry points/journeys (Quick scan vs Deep analysis)

My recommendation: **Option A** \- single Scorecard, progressive disclosure. Reduces cognitive load, doesn't force users to choose upfront.
